Employment Trends

What is One Employment Trend In Your Career and How Has It Impacted Your Thinking About Your Future?

One employment trend in aerospace engineering is a focus on rocket and launch vehicles. Canada has announced its mission to achieve sovereign launch, meaning it is trying to launch its own rockets into space rather than relying on countries like the United States for help. Because of this announcement, companies such as Nordspace are gaining funding. Nordspace specializes in creating and launching rockets, similar to other corporations such as SpaceX. Due to the surge in funding to the companies, jobs are opening up in this field. New positions are being created because these companies require a larger team to design, develop, and improve their rockets. Combined with the aging trend in Canada, such a demand for larger teams will open up far more positions than in other fields. This will impact my thinking about the future by shifting my focus towards rocket design. Initially, I wanted to get into areas such as satellite design or space systems engineering (a subfield of astronautical engineering). However, given this trend, I have decided to shift my focus to launch-oriented fields. Areas such as propulsion may be more beneficial to me. Also, this will shift my focus to which companies I will co-op with or intern at. Instead of the satellite-operating telecommunications companies, I will focus more on corporations (e.g., Reaction Dynamics) that specialize in engine design, propulsion systems, and rocket avionics. In conclusion, the current employment trend in aerospace engineering is newer job openings in rocket-related areas, which will affect what I will specialize in and which companies I will do a co-op with or intern at.
