Transferable Skills

Explain 1 essential skill required for your career. How have you developed or will develop it?

One transferable skill is oral Communication. Oral communication is an incredibly important skill in aerospace engineering, as listed by the Job Bank of Canada. Oral communication is crucial due to the collaborative nature of engineering. Engineering is made up of many different systems designed by many different engineers, departments, and design methodologies. Hence, oral communication is extremely important for understanding the status and situation of the project due to its complexity. This is not only a skill that is used in aerospace engineering, but in many other fields and situations. For example, I might need to use oral communication to communicate in a group project at school. I have done many extracurricular activities to develop my communication. One example is my student leadership volunteering experience at AGAPE Fellowship. I had to orally communicate with others to make sure we’re on the same page, as well as to contribute ideas in meetings. In addition, I require oral communication skills in my sound engineer volunteer role as well. When large events are being executed, precise, clear communication is crucial. I have gained much experience and communication skills from that experience. In conclusion, the transferable skill of oral communication is crucial to the work of engineers due to the collaboration they constantly undergo, and this skill is cultivated in my experiences as a student leader and a sound engineer volunteer.
