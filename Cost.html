What is One Financial Strategy You Will Use to Deal With the Costs?

One financial strategy I will use to fund my post-secondary education is saving. Saving is an incredibly powerful strategy for gaining the financial resources for post-secondary education. Using compound interest, streamlined and predictable processes, as well as utilizing different types of accounts, I would be able to amass a large amount of money for my university expenses. Compound interest, or interest in general, has a remarkable ability to increase your funds. Interest is the amount of money gained on the amount of money already invested in the account. For example, if I had invested 100$ at a rate of 2% annually, after 2 years it would be $110.41, and $119.51 for 10 years. Though this may look small, this is only without any contributions. Having a contribution of $10 monthly, the same investment grows to $1,447.87. This will only get exponentially greater, becoming a powerful tool for obtaining funding. In addition, saving is a streamlined and predictable process. Sources of income, such as OSAP, are subject to uncertainty from policies and politics. An example of this is the recent OSAP cuts. OSAP was cut from 85% grants to 25% grants. This uncertainty can be mitigated by savings. Savings are under your control. Though the banks technically hold your savings, bank policies rarely have sweeping changes, unlike other sources of income. Hence, using savings as a source of income gives a stable source of funding for post-secondary education. Lastly, taking advantage of the features of certain bank accounts is another key tool to fund my post-secondary education. The RESP (Registered Education Savings Plan) is an account that is specifically designed for funding post-secondary education. Not only is it tax-free compounding (tax-free with compound interest), but the government will also match a certain percentage of the amount put into the account. This tax-exempt account allows your account to grow to its fullest potential. Government contributions also relieve some of the burden on a student using this account, as well as making sure the money is within your control. RESP allows a student to blend the certainty of a savings account and the assistance of grants. In conclusion, I will use savings accounts for my post-secondary education, utilising the powerful tool of compound interest, the stability of the funding, as well as the effectiveness of RESP accounts.
